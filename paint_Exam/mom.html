MKCL Olympiad Movement(MOM) Exam

The MKCL Olympiad Movement offers a state-level competitive examination to assess the academic quality of students from the CBSE Board (English medium) and SSC Board (Marathi, Semi-English, and English medium) for grades 5th to 9th.

In addition to the school subjects, the MKCL Olympiad Movement evaluates whether students can apply the knowledge they have gained in real life and if they can use information from one subject to understand others. Students also have the opportunity to learn through assessments. They will experience solving challenging and innovative questions that will stimulate their intellect.

 

v Eligibility:

Students in the 5th, 6th, 7th, 8th, and 9th grades from CBSE Board (English medium) and SSC Board (Marathi, Semi-English, and English medium) can participate in the MKCL Olympiad Movement.

Separate exams will be conducted for each grade and each medium (Marathi, Semi-English, and English).

Students must take the exam for their respective grade.

For example, a 6th-grade student should take the MKCL Olympiad Movement exam for the 6th grade only.

Marks obtained by taking an exam for a different grade will not be considered valid.

vExam Syllabus :

MKCL Olympiad Movement Exam Syllabus:

The syllabus for the first semester for all subjects from 5th to 9th standard includes CBSE Board (English medium) and SSC Board (Marathi, Semi-English, and English medium).

In addition to Personality Development courses, the questions will cover topics such as common sense, moral values, communication skills, and soft skills (related to events in the school environment and the emotions of children in this age group). Students are not expected to read any specific textbooks or other books for these types of questions.

vExam Pattern :

MKCL Olympiad Movement Exam Pattern:

  • Computer-Based Exam:
    • Students are required to visit MKCL s Authorized Learning Center to take the exam.
    • The computer-based exam will be conducted through MKCL s AI-empowered Online Examination System with a web camera.
  • About the Exam Paper and Questions
    • For each standard, students will have to solve a single question paper comprising questions from all subjects.
    • Different questions will carry different marks based on difficulty level and educational objectives.
    • Educational objectives include Knowledge, Comprehension, Application, Analysis, Synthesis, and Evaluation/Judgement, as defined by educationist Dr. Benjamin Bloom.
    • The question paper is divided into groups of questions, with each group containing questions from all subjects. For example, for standards 5th to 7th, each group will have 7 questions, one for each subject. (This format allows students to switch between subjects, testing their cognitive flexibility)
    • Students can attempt groups one after another.
    • Students can skip difficult questions and attempt the next ones, returning to unanswered questions later within the exam duration.
    • Students can solve as many questions as possible within the 1-hour time limit.
    • The number of questions is unlimited. As students continue to solve questions, more questions will be provided, giving them the opportunity to score higher. (This format challenges students and tests their perseverance)
    • Students can end the exam at any time.
    • The exam will automatically end after 1 hour.
    • Unlike traditional exams, this exam will present and allow students to attempt a varying number of questions.

Criteria for choosing a winner:

  • Winners at the state and district levels will be selected based on the marks they have scored, the questions they have solved, the correct answers, the difficulty level of the questions, and the minimum marks required to pass.
